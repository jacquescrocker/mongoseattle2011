Developing Your App

  • Use rapid dev frameworks with active communities
    • Ruby on Rails, Sinatra, jQuery, Django, Symfony, others..
  • Use MongoDB
    • Obviously...
  • Leverage APIs, outsource as much as possible...
    http://developer.mashery.com/apis
  • Don't: Repackage Open Source Apps (Drupal, Spree, WordPress).
    Your microapp needs to be lean and mean. Avoid the bulk.

Why MongoDB?

  • really easy to get started
  • great dev tools, and learning resources
  • dead simple hosting

I just want a big dumb data store...

MongoDB allows you to....

  • focus on the product
  • throw a ton of unstructured data into the database
  • pull it out, reorganize it when needed
  • be crazy, get things done in the fastest, easiest way possible

Monetize It

  • Charge Money, srsly!
  • Target around 100 paying customers
  • Minimum 14/mo. Possibly 29/mo and higher
  • Give a free trial period (7-21 days)
  • Revenue goal: 1K - 5K/mo for each Microapp

Deploy with...

  • Forget SSH and Bash Scripts
  • Use the path of least resistance...
    • Heroku + addons
    • MongoLab

Cloud FTW!

Measure everything

  • Track all events and use this to tweak features, and figure out what people respond to most.
  • Usage metrics (not # of users, or # of pageviews) are the most important
    • translates to retention and more money long term.
  • Easy with...
    • Google Analytics
    • MixPanel

So I want to build a microapp...
what do I build?

Go the "Last Mile"

  • Millions of small apps are waiting to be built
  • Find something that at least 100 people/businesses would pay monthly
  • Build something unique, that just doesn't exist yet, for an industry that has a tools budget

Don't Build....

(for God's Sake)

  • Project Management (Basecamp Clone)
  • Bug Tracker
  • Todo List
  • Facebook, Twitter, Groupon

Build for an App Store...

  • Can provides initial dataset, user onboarding, and distribution
  • A few to check out:
    • Google Apps Enterprise
      http://www.google.com/enterprise/marketplace/
    • Intuit AppCenter
      http://appcenter.intuit.com/
    • Salesforce AppExchange
      http://appexchange.salesforce.com/
  • ... many more small niche directories for enterprise apps
  • No silver bullet, but they provide a nice opportunity.
